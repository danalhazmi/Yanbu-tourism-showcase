Yanbu Port

Yanbu Port has historically played a vital role in the economic development of the city. Due to its strategic location on the Red Sea, the port served as an important maritime gateway connecting local trade with regional routes. Over the years, it facilitated the transportation of goods, supported commercial exchange, and strengthened Yanbu’s position as a coastal trading hub.

The port handled various types of cargo and contributed significantly to the movement of goods between inland regions and international markets. Its presence enhanced economic activity and created employment opportunities for many residents.

In addition to commercial trade, fishing was a fundamental activity closely linked to the port. Local fishermen relied on traditional boats and techniques passed down through generations. Fishing not only supported daily livelihoods but also shaped the social and cultural identity of the community.

During fishing trips, fishermen often sang traditional sea chants to coordinate their work and maintain rhythm while rowing or casting nets. These songs reflected cooperation, resilience, and a deep connection to the sea. Over time, such chants became part of Yanbu’s maritime heritage and cultural memory.
