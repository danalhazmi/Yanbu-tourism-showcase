History & Background

Yanbu Industrial City was established as part of Saudi Arabia’s strategic vision to strengthen its industrial and economic sectors. Over time, it has developed into one of the Kingdom’s leading industrial hubs, particularly in petrochemical and energy-related industries.

Beyond its industrial significance, Yanbu’s coastal location has historically connected it to maritime trade routes across the Red Sea. This geographical advantage contributed to the growth of commerce and shaped the city’s cultural and social development.
